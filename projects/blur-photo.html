Overview

Precision blur, without the complexity of professional software.

Blur Photo gives photographers and casual users alike the power to apply cinematic depth-of-field blur to any photo — directly from their phone. No DSLR, no Photoshop, no subscription. Just point, tap, and blur.

The design challenge was making a technically complex AI masking engine feel as simple as drawing with your finger.

Problem Statement

Great blur is locked behind expensive gear and steep learning curves.

Portrait mode on smartphones democratised bokeh to a degree — but it only works during capture, only on compatible devices, and only for portraits. Once a photo is taken, there's no going back without professional desktop software.

Users were turning to complex apps like Photoshop or VSCO with steep learning curves, just to blur a background. The market needed a tool that was purpose-built for one thing, and did it beautifully.

"I just want my subject to pop. Why do I need a degree in Photoshop just to blur the background of one photo?"

Research & Discovery

Understanding how people actually take and edit photos.

We shadowed 18 participants through their real photo-editing workflows — from capture to posting — over two weeks. The insights reshaped our initial assumptions about what users actually needed.

Post-Capture Need

94% of users wanted to blur existing photos from their library, not just photos taken in the moment.

Masking Anxiety

Users feared making irreversible mistakes. Non-destructive editing and unlimited undo were non-negotiables.

Blur Type Confusion

Users didn't know the difference between Gaussian, lens, and tilt-shift blur. They only cared about the visual result.

Subject Detection

Auto subject detection was expected by 87% of users — manual masking was seen as a fallback, not the primary path.

The Solution

Tap your subject. Everything else is automatic.

The entire app is built around a single core gesture: tap the subject you want in focus. The AI instantly detects and masks that subject, blurs everything else, and shows you the result — all in under a second.

Users can then refine using a brush tool, adjust blur intensity with a slider, and switch between blur styles using visual previews rather than technical labels. Every decision reduces cognitive load.

"The app should do the thinking so the user can focus on the feeling. One tap to great should always be the fastest path."

Key Features

Built for humans, not photographers.

AI Subject Detection

One tap automatically isolates any subject — people, objects, pets — with pixel-accurate masking powered by on-device ML.

Blur Style Picker

Visual previews for cinematic, tilt-shift, and radial blur — no technical terminology, just results you can see.

Non-Destructive Editing

Every edit is reversible. Unlimited undo, original always preserved — users edit with confidence, not fear.

Instant Export

One-tap export to camera roll, Stories, or sharing sheet — at full resolution, with no watermarks.

Outcome & Impact

Simple design, measurable results.

The focus on reducing the path to a great result — from tap to export — paid off in every key metric. Users completed their first edit faster than any comparable app in the category.

Reflection

Simplicity is a design decision, not a limitation.

The hardest part of this project wasn't adding features — it was deciding what not to build. Every stakeholder had ideas. The AI team wanted to expose more controls. Marketing wanted more blur styles. The right call was always to ask: "Does this serve the user's primary goal?"

The version that shipped was significantly simpler than our original scope — and significantly better for it.
